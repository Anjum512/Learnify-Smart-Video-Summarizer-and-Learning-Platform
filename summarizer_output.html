The solar system is located in the Milky Way galaxy and is approximately 4.6 billion years old. It formed from the gravitational collapse of a region within a large molecular cloud. The sun contains 99.86% of the system's mass, while the remaining mass is distributed among the planets, their moons, asteroids, comets, and other celestial bodies.

The four inner terrestrial planets have dense, rocky compositions, while the four outer planets are gas giants with much larger masses but lower densities. Between Mars and Jupiter lies the asteroid belt, containing millions of rocky objects. Beyond Neptune's orbit is the Kuiper Belt, home to dwarf planets like Pluto and countless icy bodies.

Each planet has unique characteristics: Mercury has extreme temperature variations, Venus has a thick toxic atmosphere, Earth supports life with its moderate conditions, Mars shows evidence of ancient water, Jupiter has the Great Red Spot storm, Saturn possesses spectacular rings, Uranus rotates on its side, and Neptune has the fastest winds in the solar system.
